Fun facts about fruits and vegetables

Tomato

Despite its culinary classification as a vegetable, the tomato is botanically a fruit. It belongs to the nightshade family and is rich in lycopene, a powerful antioxidant.

Banana

The banana is the world's most popular fruit and is botanically classified as a berry. It grows on plants that are technically herbs and not trees.

Watermelon

This summertime favorite is 92% water, making it an excellent hydrating fruit option. It is also the official state vegetable of Oklahoma.

Avocado

Avocados are unique fruits because they contain healthy monounsaturated fats. They are also technically classified as berries and have the highest protein content of any fruit.

Pineapple

Pineapples are not just a single fruit but a cluster of many individual berries that fuse together around a central core. They contain an enzyme called bromelain, which aids digestion.

Carrot

Carrots were originally purple and yellow in color. The orange carrot we are familiar with today was developed by the Dutch in the 17th century through selective breeding.

Lemon

Lemons contain more sugar than strawberries. Despite their sour taste, lemons are one of the few fruits that are classified as acidic but produce an alkaline effect in the body.
